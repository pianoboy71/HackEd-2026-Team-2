FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

How much waste does the average person in the UK generate?

The average person in the UK produces around 400kg of waste per year. To put that into perspective, that is about 7 times the average person's body weight. This amounts to a staggering collective generation of over 26 million tonnes of waste annually by British households.

How much of UK waste is NOT being recycled?

With a 45% recycling rate, over half — approximately 14 million tonnes — of the UK's annual household waste is still not being recycled and likely ends up in landfill or incineration.

How does the UK compare to other countries in recycling?

The UK lags behind leading nations such as Germany and Austria, which recycle between 60–70% of their waste. This highlights the need for the UK to strengthen its recycling initiatives.

Why is recycling important?

Proper recycling helps protect the environment, reduces the amount of waste sent to landfill, and supports a more sustainable future for everyone.

What can I do to improve my recycling habits?

Understanding what can and cannot be recycled in your local area is a great first step. Using tools that help you identify recyclable items can make the process simpler and more effective.
